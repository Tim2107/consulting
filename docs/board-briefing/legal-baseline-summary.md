# Legal Baseline Summary

Date accessed: 2026-05-19

This board summary is derived from [legal-and-regulatory-baseline.md](../research-dossier/legal-and-regulatory-baseline.md) and [evidence-register.md](../evidence-register/evidence-register.md).

## Board-Level Conclusions

1. AI privacy governance must start with data classification and data movement, not tool selection. Evidence: CLAIM-001, CLAIM-002, CLAIM-003.
2. GDPR remains applicable whenever AI workflows process personal data; the EU AI Act adds duties but does not replace GDPR. Evidence: CLAIM-001, CLAIM-005.
3. Provider approval must include the full Provider Stack, subprocessors, Processing Location, retention, training-use terms, and transfer posture. Evidence: CLAIM-002, CLAIM-003.
4. Financial-sector consulting should treat DORA-style ICT third-party risk as a client expectation even where the company is not directly regulated as a financial entity. Evidence: CLAIM-007.
5. Employee-facing AI requires separate privacy, labor, and transparency review in Austria and Germany. Evidence: CLAIM-009, CLAIM-010.
6. AI Act transparency obligations are near-term planning work because they are scheduled for August 2026. Evidence: CLAIM-006.
7. Training and fine-tuning require a separate high-risk decision path, especially when company or client material is involved. Evidence: CLAIM-013.

## Decisions To Feed Into The Backlog

- Decide who owns DPIA trigger decisions.
- Decide minimum evidence required for Processing Location claims.
- Decide the local HR, labor-law, or works-council review path for Austria and Germany.
- Decide which financial-sector client engagements require DORA-style controls for AI use.

## Immediate Governance Position

Until the board decides otherwise:

- Keep Red Lines and Gated Exceptions in force.
- Require AI Use Case Intake for non-public data or controlled systems.
- Do not submit client contracts to AI tools.
- Treat Delivery Pipeline Systems and Automated Action as restricted unless technically gated.
