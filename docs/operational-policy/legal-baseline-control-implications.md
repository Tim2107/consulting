# Legal Baseline Control Implications

Date accessed: 2026-05-19

This operational note translates the first legal baseline into policy controls. It should be updated when the research dossier or evidence register changes.

## Required Intake Checks

- Personal data involved: maps to CLAIM-001.
- Client Confidential Data involved: maps to CLAIM-001, CLAIM-002, CLAIM-003.
- Provider Stack complete: maps to CLAIM-002.
- Processing Location known: maps to CLAIM-003.
- International transfer or non-EEA access possible: maps to CLAIM-003.
- DPIA trigger present: maps to CLAIM-004.
- Employee AI Privacy implications: maps to CLAIM-009 and CLAIM-010.
- Financial-sector DORA-style requirement possible: maps to CLAIM-007.
- NIS2 or cybersecurity supply-chain implication possible: maps to CLAIM-008.
- AI Act transparency obligation relevant: maps to CLAIM-006.
- Training or fine-tuning involved: maps to CLAIM-013.

## Required Policy Controls

- AI Use Case Intake for non-public data or controlled systems.
- Approved AI Tool Registry before broad employee rollout.
- Provider Stack and Processing Location review before approving third-party AI use.
- DPIA trigger check before workflows involving personal data, employee monitoring, profiling, or automated decision support.
- Employee AI Transparency Notice for employee-facing AI workflows.
- Client Contract Compatibility Check without contract ingestion before Client Confidential Data workflows.
- Red Lines and Gated Exceptions for high-risk uses.
- Technical Control Patterns for Delivery Pipeline Systems and Technically Gated Actions.

## Review Triggers

Update this note when:

- the EU AI Act implementation timeline changes
- DSK, DSB, EDPB, BaFin, FMA, or Commission guidance materially changes
- a provider changes retention, training, routing, subprocessor, or data-residency terms
- the company approves a new Adoption Phase
- the board changes a Red Line or Gated Exception
